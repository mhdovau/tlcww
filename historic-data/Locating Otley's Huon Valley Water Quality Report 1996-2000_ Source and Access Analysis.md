# Locating “Huon Valley Water Quality Report 1996–2000” (Otley)

## TL;DR

- The report is **Otley, H. (2001), “Huon Valley Water Quality Report 1996–2000: An aid to catchment management decisions,” Huon Valley Council, Huonville, Tasmania** — author **Helen Otley**, who worked for the Council’s Huon Healthy Rivers Project; note the formal publication date is 2001, not 2000 as the filename suggests.
- **No full PDF or scanned copy of this report is freely available anywhere online**, and no record of it appears in any searchable library catalogue (Trove, Libraries Tasmania/STORS, National Library of Australia, WorldCat).
- To obtain the complete report you will almost certainly need to contact the **Huon Valley Council** (the publisher) or **Libraries Tasmania**; the closely related and fully downloadable companion document is Christopher Bobbi’s “Water Quality of Rivers in the Huon Catchment” (1998), hosted on the Tasmanian Government (NRE) site.

## Key Findings

- The exact, verified citation is: *Otley, H. 2001. Huon Valley Water Quality Report 1996-2000: An aid to catchment management decisions. Huon Valley Council: Huonville, Australia.*  This citation recurs verbatim in the reference list of Otley’s peer-reviewed 2001 platypus paper.
- The author is **Helen Otley** (also published as Helen M. Otley), affiliated at the time with the **Huon Healthy Rivers Project, Huon Valley Council, PO Box 210, Huonville, Tasmania 7109**.  She is a platypus/freshwater ecologist; her companion *Australian Zoologist* paper, drawing on the same Huon catchment community fieldwork, reported that “the species was found to be common and sighted in waterbodies throughout the Huon River catchment. Fewer platypuses were sighted in the eastern part of the catchment, where rainfall is lower and mudstone is the dominant geology.”
- The report sits within the broader **Huon Catchment Healthy Rivers Project** run by the Huon Valley Council, drawing on community **Waterwatch** monitoring data and Landcare group involvement, complemented by state monitoring (Bobbi’s catchment study) and CSIRO’s Huon Estuary Study.
- Despite extensive searching, the report is **grey literature** (an in-house council report) that was never digitised for public web access. The Huon Valley Council’s own “Other Reports and Studies” web page lists numerous studies but does NOT include the Otley water quality report.
- The references the user mentioned (“Bobbi 1998”; “Meyer and Lynch 2000”) and the catchments listed (Castle Forbes Bay Rivulet, Dover Rivulet, Esperance River, Kermandie River, Agnes Rivulet, Supplices Creek, etc.) are all consistent with the Huon catchment water-quality literature of that period, confirming the report’s subject matter and scope.

## Details

### What the report is

The document is a synthesis of roughly five years (1996–2000) of water-quality monitoring across the Huon Valley’s sub-catchments, prepared to guide catchment management. Parameters covered match standard Waterwatch protocols of the era: water temperature, turbidity, electrical conductivity, pH, faecal bacteria (E. coli) and ortho-phosphate. The structure described by the user (a “Results” section 4 with subsections 4.10, 4.11, etc., each likely covering an individual rivulet/creek) is consistent with a site-by-site reporting format.

The report was one of several outputs of the **Huon Catchment Healthy Rivers Project**, a Huon Valley Council initiative aimed at building a scientific knowledge base for long-term planning and management of the region’s waterways.  Earlier project outputs include “Water Quality Assessment Report” (1996) and “Catchment Management Plan” (1997), both also published by the Huon Valley Council. 

### Where it can (and cannot) be found online

- **Full PDF / download:** Not available. No open-access copy, repository copy, or scan was located on the Council site, Tasmanian Government sites, academic repositories (Academia.edu, ResearchGate), or general web search.
- **Library catalogues:** No catalogue record was found in Trove, Libraries Tasmania, the STORS digital repository, the National Library of Australia catalogue, or WorldCat. (These catalogues use database-driven search interfaces; a definitive confirmation would require a librarian to run an interactive search, but no trace appeared in any web search.)
- **Council website:** The Huon Valley Council “Reports and Publications → Other Reports and Studies” page (huonvalley.tas.gov.au) lists flood studies, feasibility studies and recent recreational water quality sampling reports (2020–2025), but does not list the 2001 Otley report.

### Closely related material that IS freely available online

- **Bobbi, C.J. (1998), “Water Quality of Rivers in the Huon Catchment,” Report series WRA 98/01, Tasmania Department of Primary Industry and Fisheries, Hobart** — a State of Rivers-style report covering the same catchments and parameters (a “comprehensive water monitoring program at 13 sites in the Huon region,” with summer and winter catchment surveys in January and June 1997). It is downloadable as a PDF from the Tasmanian Department of Natural Resources and Environment: `https://nre.tas.gov.au/Documents/Huon-River-Catchment-SOR-Water-Quality.pdf`. This is almost certainly the “Bobbi (1998)” reference cited in the Otley report.
- **CSIRO Huon Estuary Study (2000)** — final report to FRDC, downloadable at `https://www.cmar.csiro.au/research/huonestuary/report/CSIRO_FRDC_HuonEstuaryStudy2000.PDF`.
- **EPA Tasmania, “Huon Valley Catchments” Protected Environmental Values final paper** — `https://epa.tas.gov.au/documents/huon_valley_catchments_final_paper.pdf`.
- **Otley’s peer-reviewed companion paper** drawing on the same Huon catchment work: Otley, H. (2001), “The use of a community-based survey to determine the distribution of the platypus *Ornithorhynchus anatinus* in the Huon River catchment, southern Tasmania,” *Australian Zoologist* 31(4): 632–641, DOI 10.7882/AZ.2001.010  (abstract and reference list at meridian.allenpress.com; a copy is on Academia.edu at `https://www.academia.edu/1390771/`).

## Recommendations

1. **Contact the Huon Valley Council directly first** — it is the publisher and the most likely holder of the master copy. Phone (03) 6264 0300  or email **[hvc@huonvalley.tas.gov.au](mailto:hvc@huonvalley.tas.gov.au)** (40 Main Street / PO Box 210, Huonville TAS 7109); ask the Natural Resource Management (NRM) Unit specifically, as the NRM/Waterwatch program is the lineal descendant of the Huon Healthy Rivers Project that produced the report.
1. **Submit a reference enquiry to Libraries Tasmania** (libraries.tas.gov.au; email [libraries.tas@libraries.tas.gov.au](mailto:libraries.tas@libraries.tas.gov.au)) and ask them to check the state collection and the STORS repository for the title and author Helen Otley. As a Tasmanian government/council publication it may exist as a legal-deposit item even though no online record surfaced.
1. **Ask the Upper Huon Valley Wildlife Monitoring Landcare Group / Landcare Tasmania** — this group ran a 2022 freshwater quality information session reviewing historic Huon data  and may hold or know the whereabouts of the Otley report. Contact via Landcare Tasmania (landcaretas.org.au).
1. **Try Chris Bobbi directly** — Bobbi was a presenter at the 2022 Landcare session  and is the author of the freely available companion state report; as a long-time Huon freshwater scientist he may have a copy.
1. **If you only need the underlying data and findings**, the freely downloadable Bobbi (1998) report and the CSIRO Huon Estuary Study (2000) cover the same catchments, parameters and time period and will substitute for much of the content.

- **Benchmark that would change the recommendation:** if a Trove/WorldCat/Libraries Tasmania record surfaces with a holding library, request via inter-library loan instead of contacting the Council.

## Caveats

- The filename “Otley_2000_Huon_water_quality_report” implies a 2000 date, but every verified bibliographic citation dates the published report to **2001**; the title’s “1996–2000” refers to the data coverage period, not the publication year. It is possible a 2000 draft circulated before formal 2001 publication.
- The “no catalogue record” finding is based on the absence of any hit across multiple targeted searches; the major catalogues use JavaScript/database search interfaces that could not be queried directly, so a manual librarian search could still surface a record.
- The exact internal structure (sections 4.10, 4.11, etc.) and the specific “Meyer and Lynch (2000)” reference could not be independently verified online because the full text is not available; these details come from the user’s own copy and are consistent with the report’s known scope.
